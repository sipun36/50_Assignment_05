about nitw

The history of the National Institute of Technology, Warangal (NITW) began on October 10, 1959, when Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru laid the foundation stone for what was then the Regional Engineering College (REC), Warangal. This marked the establishment of the first NIT in India, a chain of which was planned across the country. In September 2002, the institute was renamed the National Institute of Technology (NIT), Warangal, and was granted the status of a Deemed University. For over six decades, NIT Warangal has grown into a prominent institution of national importance, recognized for its strong academic environment and significant contributions to engineering and technology.
